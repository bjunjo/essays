What I Learned From My First Job

On ownership, time, and the things they don't teach you.

Most people confuse being busy with making progress. They do more. They fill the calendar. They optimize the routine. But the real question is never "how do I do this better?" It is "should I be doing this at all?"

Searching is harder than doing. Doing gives you the feeling of movement. Searching forces you to sit still and ask uncomfortable questions. What do I actually want? What is the highest-leverage thing I could be working on right now? What am I avoiding by staying busy?

The people who change their lives are not the ones who work the hardest. They are the ones who paused long enough to find the right direction. Then they worked hard.

Do less. Search more. Find the thing worth doing. Then do it completely.
