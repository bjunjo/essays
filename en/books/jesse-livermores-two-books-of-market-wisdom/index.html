Jesse Livermore's Two Books of Market Wisdom

by Jesse Lauriston Livermore, Edwin Lefèvre, and Richard Demille Wyckoff

My note

The patience Jesse Livermore honed—waiting for the right moment to move without fear of retreat—resonates deeply with how I think about capital allocation and Bitcoin’s slow, steady paradigm shift. It’s a reminder that mastering the game isn’t about constant action, but about understanding your edge and having the discipline to hold your ground. Thought you’d appreciate how timeless these lessons feel, even in today’s world of digital money and evolving markets.

He was among the first to recognize that speculating’s greatest challenges came not from a tough market but from emotional responses such as impatience, hope, and fear.
