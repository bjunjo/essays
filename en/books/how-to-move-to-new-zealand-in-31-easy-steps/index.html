How to Move to New Zealand in 31 Easy Steps

by Sara Dawn Johnson

My note

Thinking about how sovereignty extends beyond finances to where and how we choose to live, this book lays out a clear, practical path to embedding yourself in a new ecosystem—one that values entrepreneurship and long-term opportunity. It’s a reminder that reallocating capital isn’t just about assets, but also about investing in environments that align with our values and freedom. You might find its grounded approach to immigration surprisingly empowering, especially when considering the bigger picture of where we want to build our future.
