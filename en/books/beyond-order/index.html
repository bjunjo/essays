Beyond Order

by Jordan B. Peterson

My note

Navigating complexity feels a lot like allocating capital—both demand patience, humility, and a willingness to challenge outdated frameworks. Peterson’s reminder that true sanity involves understanding and working within social realities, while still daring to offer creative alternatives, resonates deeply with how I think about long-term sovereignty and growth. It’s a steady call to balance order with the courage to rethink what’s broken.

Rule III warns of the dangers of avoiding the information (vital to the continual rejuvenation of the psyche) signaled by the emergence of negative emotions such as pain, anxiety, and fear.

Rule I describes the relationship between stable, predictable social structures and individual psychological health, and makes the case that such structures need to be updated by creative people if they are to retain their vitality.

When you are visited by chaos and swallowed up; when nature curses you or someone you love with illness; or when tyranny rends asunder something of value that you have built, it is salutary to know the rest of the story. We ignore that addition to the story at our peril, because life is so difficult that losing sight of the heroic part of existence could cost us everything.
