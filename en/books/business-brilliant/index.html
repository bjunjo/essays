Business Brilliant

by Lewis Schiff

My note

Knowing when and how to ask for more isn’t just about salary—it’s about owning your value and refusing to settle for less, something I see as crucial not just in work but in life and capital allocation. This book’s take on overcoming fear to claim what you’re worth feels like a reminder that true sovereignty starts with standing firm, even when it’s uncomfortable. Thought you’d appreciate the way it challenges the quiet compromises most of us make every day.

Deci hasn’t coined a catchy name for what’s going on here, although he commonly refers to it as the “undermining effect.” For our purposes, I’d like to call it something else: Paycheck Paralysis.

Work environments that are driven by monetary incentives and close supervision tend to sap worker enthusiasm for tasks that they might otherwise find interesting.
