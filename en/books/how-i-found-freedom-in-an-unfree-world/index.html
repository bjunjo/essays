My note

Freedom isn’t just about what you own or where you live — it’s about the space to be honest with yourself and set boundaries that actually align with who you are. This book reminded me that true sovereignty starts with clear choices and accepting the costs, not chasing illusions of unlimited gain. It feels like a quiet blueprint for living deliberately, something I keep coming back to in both capital and life.
