The Power of Starting Something Stupid

by Richie Norton and Natalie Norton

My note

There’s something powerful in treating “stupid ideas” like active projects instead of distant dreams—especially when long-term thinking can easily turn into deferred living. This book reminds me that true financial sovereignty isn’t just about saving money, but about investing in ourselves and the messy, uncertain leaps that spark real change. It’s a call to act small but think ₿ig, something I hope we both keep close.

CLARIFYING PRINCIPLES RULE #1: Saving and investing money for the future = GOOD RULE #2: Saving dreams (stupid ideas) for the future = BAD

The ability to retire is great! The unintended consequence of retirement planning, however, is that while people plan for future financial savings and investments (a good thing), they get it all mixed up with saving their dreams for later (a bad thing). Save your money, not your dreams!

The concept of retirement planning is a good thing that has simply gone too far. Sadly, retirement planning, in many circumstances, has become nothing more than planned procrastination.

the years rolled by, these individuals began to realize that they’d deferred more than their taxes; they’d deferred their lives.

The pursuit of prosperity shouldn’t stop you from the pursuit of happiness.

The poor souls who buy into the idea of the Deferred Life Plan are bound to get caught in an activity trap—constantly doing, but never achieving.

“When it’s time to die, let us not discover that we have never lived.” —Henry David Thoreau

Opportunities will come and go, but if you do nothing about them, so will you.

How many ideas, opportunities, businesses, and lives are squandered because we mistakenly suppress those so-called “stupid ideas.”

We all want to be smart. We’re scared of failure. Scared of falling behind. Scared of being foolish. Scared of looking stupid. No one wants any of that.

“Everything can be taken from a man but one thing: the last of the human freedoms—to choose one’s attitude in any given set of circumstances, to choose one’s own way.” —Viktor E. Frankl, author of Man’s Search for Meaning

Don’t wait. Start stuff. Live to start your stupid ideas, and start to live a life without regret—a life filled with meaning, freedom, happiness, fun, authenticity, and influence. After all, now is, in all actuality, the only time you’re truly guaranteed. Life is too short not to start something stupid.
