Effortless

by Greg Mckeown

My note

Finding ways to blend joy with essential work feels like a quiet revolution against the old script of hustle and grind—something that resonates deeply with how I think about capital allocation and long-term freedom. The idea that momentum grows naturally when effort becomes effortless reminds me that building sovereignty, whether financial or personal, is less about force and more about smart rhythm. Hope this sparks some playful strategies for us to rethink how we approach the hard stuff together.

Effortless Inversion means looking at problems from the opposite perspective. It means asking, “What if this could be easy?” It means learning to solve problems from a state of focus, clarity, and calm. It means getting good at getting things done by putting in less effort.

To invert means to turn an assumption or approach upside down, to work backward, to ask, “What if the opposite were true?”
