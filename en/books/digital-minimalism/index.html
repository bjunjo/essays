Digital Minimalism

by Cal Newport

My note

Choosing where to focus attention feels a lot like capital allocation—each minute spent is an investment in your own mental portfolio. Digital Minimalism reminds me that guarding solitude isn’t just about cutting noise, but about cultivating the long-term clarity and sovereignty that ultimately shape how we think and act. Thoughtful restraint in daily input feels like the quiet work behind any big paradigm shift.

As I mentioned earlier in this chapter, however, it’s a mistake to think of the digital declutter as only a detox experience. The goal is not to simply give yourself a break from technology, but to instead spark a permanent transformation of your digital life.

The Digital Declutter Process Put aside a thirty-day period during which you will take a break from optional technologies in your life. During this thirty-day break, explore and rediscover activities and behaviors that you find satisfying and meaningful. At the end of the break, reintroduce optional technologies into your life, starting from a blank slate. For each technology you reintroduce, determine what value it serves in your life and how specifically you will use it so as to maximize this value.
