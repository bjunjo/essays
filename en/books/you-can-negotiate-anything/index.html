You Can Negotiate Anything

by Herb Cohen

My note

Negotiation isn’t just about deals—it’s about understanding the subtle power dynamics and information flows that shape every interaction, much like navigating the shifting paradigms of capital allocation. This book reminded me how raising my aspiration level and questioning assumptions are essential, whether I’m managing Bitcoin or life itself. Thought you’d appreciate how it ties into thinking long-term and owning your decisions, just like we do.

Of course, I’m referring to Jesus Christ and Socrates. By my definition, they were negotiators. They were Win-Win ethical negotiators, and they were power people. In fact, both of them deliberately used many of the collaborative approaches I will teach you through this book.

Power. The other side always seems to have more power and authority than you think you have.

Time. The other side doesn’t seem to be under the same kind of organizational pressure, time constraints, and restrictive deadlines you feel you’re under.

Information. The other side seems to know more about you and your needs than you know about them and their needs.
