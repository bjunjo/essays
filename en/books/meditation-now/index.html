Meditation Now

by S.N. Goenka

My note

Meditation Now reminds me that true freedom isn’t just financial or external, but rooted in mastering the mind’s chaos and finding lasting peace within. It’s a powerful complement to thinking long-term about life and capital—because without inner clarity, no external sovereignty can hold. Elsa, I see this as a guide not just for stillness, but for building a foundation that supports everything we value.

My highlights

49 highlights from Kindle. These are the lines I stopped at.

‘I am earning for myself but I am also earning for others.’

It is a basic human need that everyone wants to live a happy life. For this, one has to experience real happiness. The so-called happiness that one experiences by having money, power, and indulging in sensual pleasures is not real happiness. It is very fragile, unstable and fleeting. For real happiness, for lasting stable happiness, one has to make a journey deep within oneself and get rid of all the unhappiness stored in the deeper levels of the mind.

a human being is a human being; the human mind is the human mind.

Similarly by the law of nature, if I keep my mind pure, full of love and compassion, I enjoy the kingdom of heaven within here and now.

Whatever one’s religion, or tradition, or country, when one breaks the law of nature and generates negativity in the mind, one is bound to suffer.

When I generate anger, hatred, illwill, or animosity, I am the first victim of my anger. I am the first victim of the hatred or animosity that I have generated within.

When you experience the truth about yourself, within yourself, at the experiential level, the problems of life find their solution.

All the sages, saints, and seers of the world have advised, “Know thyself.” That means not merely knowing at the intellectual level, or accepting at the emotional or devotional level, but realizing by experience at the actual level.

Enlightened persons have therefore said, “First find peace within yourself.”

I cannot because I have no peace myself.

To me it seems that if we want peace in human society, we cannot ignore individuals. If there is no peace in the mind of the individual, I do not understand how there can be real peace in the world. If I have an agitated mind, always full of anger, hatred, illwill and animosity, how can I give peace to the world?

Religion is religion only when it unites; when it divides us, it is nothing.

The Buddha used to say that all good and bad qualities are found in all the classes of society.

Sayagyi U Ba Khin, used to say, “Purity of mind is the greatest common denominator of all religions.” No one with a pure mind can indulge in such dastardly acts.

We are our own masters. Develop that mastery of yourself and you will become a happy person.

One hundred percent of the cause of our happiness is within ourselves, not outside.

The purpose of Vipassana is to purify the mind, to live a good life.

That has become the habit pattern of this deepest level of the mind. And unless we change this deepest level, all other things that we are doing at the surface are only temporary. They cannot help. If the root is unhealthy, the whole tree is unhealthy.

The root level is what we call the unconscious mind. It is very blind. It will not listen to any advice from the intellect. It will not listen to any advice. It will just keep on reacting. Whenever it feels something pleasant, it will react with craving, clinging. When it feels something unpleasant, it will react with aversion, hatred.

Sayagyi U Ba Khin, who became my teacher. He said, “All these are games of the conscious level of the mind, the surface level, whereas your habit pattern lies at the root level.”

Because from birth, when we first opened our eyes and started looking outside, we have been giving all importance to things outside.

We have to be firm and very stern, and very strong in opposing them, but deep inside there must be only love and compassion, no hatred. Hatred and anger cannot solve any problem.

People forget that when they generate negativity they are not only harming others, they are harming themselves.

Right now, people don’t know where their real self-interest lies, and they harm themselves. People need to be compassionate toward themselves.

people should think about themselves. I want everyone to be selfish, but selfish in a proper sense.

You experience, yourself, the truth, and you find that, “Yes, it is good for me, good for others.” Then you accept it.

Each individual has to enlighten oneself, and then only should one accept the truth—not because the teacher says so. Not because the Buddha said so. Not because the Christ said so. Not because the scriptures said so.

You experience the practice yourself. If you find it is good for you, then accept it. Don’t accept the word of a guru unless you have experienced the teaching and found it helpful to you and helpful to others.

Each individual is one’s own master.

Rich or poor, everyone is living an egoistic, self-centered life, generating negativities, becoming miserable. If this technique is given to these people, they will become happy. So one feels like sharing it.

I got so much from this technique. I was a very rich, very angry person, and very unhappy. I had a lot of problems in my life. And this technique took me out of those problems as if I had a totally new birth.

The body may be very healthy, and yet if your mind is not healthy, you can’t keep your body healthy. It will become unhealthy.

Doing Vipassana is like doing physical exercises to keep your body healthy. Here is a mental exercise to keep the mind healthy, which is much more important.

There is no cult or sect or religion involved in Vipassana.

By the practice of Vipassana you go to the depth of your mind—where the actual misery starts because of these negativities, where the actual happiness is experienced because of the absence of these negativities—and once you start experiencing these things for yourself, a change automatically happens in your mind.

by practicing, practicing; experiencing, experiencing, one starts changing the behavior pattern of the mind.

It won’t wait until after death and take you to hell. You suffer the pangs of hell now. You become so miserable. Similarly if you keep your mind pure—full of love, compassion and goodwill—it starts giving the reward here and now.

If you defile your mind nature, the law of nature, starts punishing you then and there.

You are experiencing it. And when you experience it directly, the next time you will be more careful not to generate such negativity: “Look, this is like fire. If I generate anger, it burns.

This reality is not to be accepted at the intellectual level, not to be accepted at the devotional level; it has to be experienced by each individual.

Purity of the mind makes us feel very happy, peaceful and harmonious. We may belong to any community, any religion, any sect or none at all. Vipassana is beyond all religions, beyond all sects, beyond all beliefs, beyond all dogmas or cults.

Similarly, if the mind is free from these defilements—if one does not generate anger and the mind is free from negativity, if the mind is pure—one will notice that the mind becomes full of love, full of compassion and goodwill.

The law of the nature does not discriminate, does not favor. This is truth, truth eternal—for everybody, all the time, past, present and future.

Anybody who puts a hand in fire gets burned. Fire won’t discriminate for a Hindu or a Muslim or a Christian or a Jew. The defilements of the mind act the same way: if you generate anger, hatred, ill will, animosity, passion, fear, ego, worry, anxiety—any impurity in the mind—it will make you miserable, it will make everyone miserable.

So truths which are based on devotion, or truths which are based on intellect, will always differ. They cannot be the same. But truth based on actual experience will remain the same.

“I have great devotion for Jesus Christ,” and I will say, “Whatever Jesus Christ said is truth.” These are devotional games and they also vary from person to person.

Vipassana is not an intellectual game. It is also not an emotional or devotional game. This is another kind of truth that human beings create: “I have great devotion in Buddha, so whatever Buddha says is the truth.”

Mr. Goenka has always emphasized that the practical application of meditation is what will enable human beings to achieve inner as well as outer peace.

Mr. Goenka believes and teaches that for peace outside (among nations, among different communities) there must be peace inside. Individuals must learn the “art of living “in order to live peaceful lives.
