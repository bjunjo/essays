Only the Paranoid Survive

by Andrew S. Grove

My note

Recognizing when the ground shifts beneath you—and having the courage to adapt—is the real test of long-term sovereignty. This book reminds me that clarity and conviction aren’t just for careers but for every pivot we face, whether in capital or life. Letting go of old identities to embrace new paradigms feels like the only way forward in a world that won’t wait.

As I said, this book is also about careers. As businesses are created on new foundations or are restructured to operate in a new environment, careers are broken or accelerated. I hope this book will give you some ideas of how you can shepherd your own career through these difficult times.

You need to accept ownership of your career, your skills and the timing of your moves. It is your responsibility to protect this personal business of yours from harm and to position it to benefit from the changes in the environment. Nobody else can do that for you.

The sad news is, nobody owes you a career. Your career is literally your business. You own it as a sole proprietor. You have one employee: yourself. You are in competition with millions of similar businesses: millions of other employees all over the world.
