Courage

by Osho

My note

Facing the fear of losing what’s false feels like a necessary step toward real freedom—something I see echoed both in Bitcoin’s promise and in personal growth. Courage, as Osho puts it, isn’t about loud bravado but about quietly shedding the ego and false identities to embrace a deeper, more sovereign self. It’s a reminder that true strength lies in the willingness to be alone with ourselves, beyond the noise of the crowd.

My highlights

120 highlights from Kindle. These are the lines I stopped at.

There is no need to be afraid. You can lose only that which has to be lost. And it is good to lose it soon—because the longer it stays, the stronger it becomes.

The same crowd, they are trying to make their false identity more consolidated.

Not the Hindus, not the Mohammedans, not the Christians—they are against searching.

Only the brave, the courageous, the people who have guts, can be religious. Not the churchgoers—these are the cowards.

Slowly slowly, you start feeling a new quality to yourself, a new aliveness, a new beauty, a new intelligence—which is not borrowed from anybody, which is growing within you.

Meditation is just a courage to be silent and alone.

All meditative techniques are a help to destroy the false. They don’t give you the real—the real cannot be given.

The crowd is an essential for the false self to exist. The moment it is lonely, you start freaking out. This is where one should understand a little bit of meditation.

You immediately start doing something just to keep yourself engaged. If there are no people, at least there is some action, so the false remains engaged and does not start disappearing.

“I am losing myself, my senses, my sanity, my mind, everything” … because the self that has been given to you by others consists of all these things—it looks like you will go insane.

And even a good intention in the hands of an unconscious person turns out to be poisonous.

Their intentions were good, just their awareness was nil. They were not conscious people—your parents, your teachers, your priests, your politicians—they were not conscious people, they were unconscious.

Habits die hard. So many years of living in a false personality imposed by people whom you loved, whom you respected … and they were not intentionally doing anything bad to you.

Only those who are ready to become nobodies are able to love. Only those who are ready to become nothing, utterly empty of themselves, are able to receive the gift of love from the beyond.

Love needs great courage. It needs great courage because it needs the sacrifice of the ego.

And we accept all this nonsense! And we accept it without any resistance because we have been conditioned to accept these things, we have been taught that this is how things are.

Christians talk about love and they have been creating wars, crusades. Mohammedans talk about love and they have been creating jihads, religious wars. Hindus talk about love, but you can look into their scriptures—they are full of hate, hate for other religions.

Hate is unnatural. Love is a state of health; hate is a state of illness.

If man is left alone, unpoisoned, then love will be simple, very simple. There will be no problem. It will be just like water flowing downward, or vapor rising upward, trees blossoming, birds singing.

Love sharpens intelligence, fear dulls it.

Life seems to be dangerous to him, because to live means you will have to love—how can you live? Just as the body needs breathing to live, the soul needs love to live.

A man full of fear can only hate—hate is a natural outcome of fear. A man full of fear is also full of anger, and a man full of fear is more against life than for life. Death seems to be a restful state to the fear-filled man. The fearful man is suicidal, he is life-negative.

Love gives courage, love takes all fear away—and the oppressors depend on your fear.

And the secret is the same: destroy love.

Hence the priest and the politician, these two, are the enemies of humanity. They are in a conspiracy, because the politician wants to rule your body and the priest wants to rule your soul.

The loving heart is full of God. There is no need for any search, there is no need for any prayer, there is no need to go to any temple, to any priest.

Because you are not blissful, because you are not peaceful, because you are not ecstatic, you are searching for God—otherwise, who bothers? Who cares? If your life is a dance, God has been attained already.

It is because love is missing that you are searching for God. God is nothing but a substitute for your missing love.

If man has love, there can’t be nations; nations exist on hate.

And the best way to destroy man is to destroy his spontaneity of love.

When you are anxious, the mind is there too much. When you are not anxious, the mind is not so much.

The more fearless a person is, the less mind he uses.

the mind is the cause of helping people to become closed. The mind is very afraid of opening because the mind exists basically out of fear.

Desire comes out of discontent.

You desire because you don’t have. You desire because you think that if you have something it will give you contentment.

You may be surprised when I say to you that love makes you desireless, but desire comes with discontent.

Deep contentment is the visible sign of love. Whenever a person is in love, he is in deep contentment. Love cannot be seen, but contentment, the deep satisfaction around him

Meditate on it. And then allow the other to enter you, invite the other to enter you.

Nobody is against you! Even if you feel somebody is against you, he too is not against you—because everybody is concerned with himself, not with you.

And then you can invite the other to penetrate you to the very core.

When you are not afraid, then there is nothing to hide; then you can be open, then you can withdraw all boundaries.

To an extent you allow the other, but then the wall comes up and everything stops.

Fear-oriented living can never lead you into deep relationship. You remain afraid, and the other cannot be allowed, cannot be allowed to penetrate you to your very core.

There are two types of living: one fear-oriented, one love-oriented.

You can allow somebody to enter to your center only when you are not afraid, when you are not fearful.

Don’t become knowledgeable, don’t go on labeling things. That’s what knowledgeability is—the great art of labeling everything, categorizing everything.

In fear one becomes closed, in love one opens. In fear one doubts, in love one trusts. In fear one is left lonely. In love one disappears; hence there is no question of loneliness at all.

The real opposite of love is fear.

Hate is love standing upside down,

A formal relationship is not a relationship at all.

Talk to God as you would talk to a wise friend. But don’t bring formalities in.

nowhere is there any opportunity available where you are allowed to love—and love in freedom.

What is fear, then? Fear is a feeling of no contact with existence. Let this be the definition of fear: a state of no contact with existence is fear.

When you don’t love out of your fullness of heart, you are bargaining. You want to force the other person to do something for you, only then will you love;

Once they have settled, once they have started taking each other for granted, then conditions are being imposed: “You should be like this, you should behave like that, only then will I love”—as if love is a bargain.

Whenever love happens it is a part of freedom; then soon you bring that fresh air into your house and everything goes stale, dirty.

Everybody creates conditions; love disappears.

Fear is nothing but absence of love.

When nobody is there at least your misery is with you—one is married to it. And it is a long, long marriage; you have remained married to misery for many lives.

Time is not needed—you can simply jump out of it!

You have done it and you can undo it. And there is no need to wait, to delay.

Nobody, no other force, is doing anything to you. It is you and only you.

I am a victim! I am being tossed from here and there by greater forces than me and I cannot do anything. So at the most I can cry about being miserable and become more miserable by crying.”

A man really becomes a man when he accepts total responsibitity–he is responsible for whatsoever he is. This is the first courage, the greatest courage.

nobody is holding you there; it is you who has decided to remain in that prison of misery.

Memory is your record. You are the one who is looking into the record, you are not the memory itself.

Memory is a content in consciousness, you are consciousness itself.

This “I” is afraid, not really you. The being has no fear, but the ego has fear, because the ego is very very afraid of dying.

Memory creates the knot, the complex called “I,” the ego. And naturally this false entity called “I” is continuously afraid of death. That’s why you are afraid of the new.

Act without any will or direction or impulse from the past—and that is to act meditatively. Act spontaneously. Let the moment be decisive.

Mind is always old. Mind is never new; it is the accumulation of the past.

How do we become new? We do not become new of ourselves. Newness comes from the beyond, say from God.

Your name is not you, neither is your fame you.

Your name and fame go on growing and you think you are growing? You are simply deceiving yourself.

Your bank balance goes on growing and you start thinking that you are growing.

because you cannot grow you have to find substitute growths. You cannot grow, but your bank balance can grow—that’s a substitute.

How can you grow if you are a coward? With each new opportunity you shrink back, you close your eyes. How can you grow? How can you be? You only pretend to be.

And the world is full of cowards, hence people have stopped growing.

Courage is needed, and not ordinary courage; extraordinary courage is needed.

Once known it has become repetitive; once known it has become boring, monotonous. You want to get rid of it. You want to explore, you want to adventure.

And you cannot keep rejecting it either, because the old has not given you yet what you seek. The old has been promising, but the promises have not been fulfilled.

You have lived in one way, you have thought in one way, you have made a comfortable life out of your beliefs. Then something new knocks on the door.

The new is unfamiliar. It may be the friend, it may be the enemy, who knows? And there is no way to know! The only way to know is to allow it; hence the apprehension, the fear.

Then one knows what it means to burn your life’s torch from both ends together. And even a single moment of that intensity is more gratifying than the whole eternity of mediocre living.

Life can only be lived dangerously—there is no other way to live it. It is only through danger that life attains to maturity, growth. One needs to be an adventurer, always ready to risk the known for the unknown. And once one has tasted the joys of freedom and fearlessness, one never repents because then one knows what it means to live at the optimum.

So always listen for the unknown. And gather courage to move into the unknown.

In a better world, every family will learns from children. You are in such a hurry to teach them. Nobody seems to learn from them, and they have much to teach you.

ALWAYS REMEMBER, TRUST IN THE UNKNOWN. The know is the mind. The unknown cannot be the mind. It may be something else but it cannot be the mind. One thing certain about the mind is that mind is the accumulated known.

but advice helps nobody. In fact, only fools give advice, and only fools take advice.

Peace is when there is no mind.

In fact, if a man knows what peace is, and what mind is, he cannot write a book entitled Peace of Mind, because mind is the cause of all unpeace, all restlessness.

Success takes all peace from your mind. It simply sucks everything that is significant in life: peace, silence, joy, love.

Knowing this simple principle, that wherever there is demand there is supply,

Once you succeed then you know that you have been cheated by the world, by the people, by the society.

Nothing fails like success. Success is significant only if you are a failure.

One rich man, very rich, became in the end very frustrated, which is a natural outcome of all success.

You don’t know who you are, so some false identity is needed as a substitute. Your possessions give you that false identity.

You are your name, you are your fame, you are your prestige, your power. But other than these, who are you?

Without possessions, success, fame—who are you? You don’t know.

Authenticity is not going to help you. Honesty is not going to help you. Truthfulness is not going to help you.

To increase that possession—to have more money, to have more power, to conquer more lands—you were doing things that even you cannot say were right. You were lying, you were dishonest. You were having hundreds of faces. You were not true even for a single moment to anybody or to yourself;

Wisdom comes through the meeting of the heart and the intellect. And once you have learned the art of how to create a synchronicity between your heartbeats and the workings of your intellect, you have the whole secret in your hands, the master key to open all the mysteries.

how can you trust anybody or anything if you don’t trust yourself?

Discover trust within yourself, don’t cultivate it. Go deeper into your being, to the very source of your being, and discover it.

Trust cannot be “cultivated”—and never try to cultivate it; that is what has been done by the whole of humanity. Cultivated trust becomes belief.

Everybody has to pass through doubt. Long is the journey, dark is the night.

Drop beliefs. The fear will be there—because if you drop belief, doubt arises.

Trust is personal; belief is social.

I say it is good to be an atheist rather than a theist, a pseudotheist.

You cannot have a map of it. It is not measurable, it is an unmeasurable mystery. Don’t ask for explanations.

Life is not stagnant—it is a dynamism, it is a process.

By the time the map is available it is useless, life has changed its tracks. Life has started playing a new game.

Everything else goes on changing, so you cannot have a map; by the time the map is ready it is already out of date.

There is nothing old under the sun, I say to you: everything is new. It is a tremendous dynamism, an absolute movement. Only change is permanent, only change never changes.

Life is not a problem. To look at it as a problem is to take a wrong step. It is a mystery to be lived, loved, experienced.

Don’t try to understand life. Live it! Don’t try to understand love. Move into love. Then you will know—and that knowing will come out of your experiencing.

Trust needs great intelligence, courage, integrity.

Doubt is out of fear. Doubt is out of a kind of insecurity in your own intelligence.

when there is utter silence as far as you are concerned—when there is nobody in you, just a pure silence, a virgin silence, undistracted, undisturbed by logic, thought, emotion, feeling—that is the moment of meditation. Mind is gone, and when mind is gone mystery enters.

Dancing, don’t do it technically; technique is irrelevant. You may be technically correct and yet you will miss the whole joy of it. Dissolve yourself in the dance, become the dance, forget about the dancer.
