A Random Walk Down Wall Street

by Burton G. Malkiel

My note

Watching markets chase the next big thing only to crash hard reminds me how vital it is to stay grounded and think long-term—something Malkiel’s take on bubbles and IPOs really drives home. It’s a sharp reminder that true financial sovereignty comes from patience and skepticism, not hype. Holding Bitcoin and choosing where to allocate capital feels like a quiet rebellion against those castles in the air.
