Money Mischief

by Milton Friedman

My note

Money Mischief really nails how fragile money’s trust is, and why limiting its supply matters deeply—something I’ve thought a lot about with Bitcoin’s fixed issuance. It reminds me that true financial sovereignty isn’t just about holding assets, but understanding the forces behind money’s value and how communities ultimately choose what to trust. Feels like a quiet call to focus on real wealth, not just the numbers on a screen.
