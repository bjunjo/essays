Reframe Your Brain

by Scott Adams and Joshua Lisec

My note

Shifting how we frame setbacks feels like capital allocation for the mind—invest energy in what works instead of dwelling on what doesn’t. This book reminded me that long-term growth, whether in Bitcoin or self, comes from reinforcing small wins and staying curious about better ways to play the game. Thought you’d appreciate its mix of practical mindset hacks and a fresh, less judgmental way to rewire how we think.

Reframes are easy to learn, easy to remember, often influential, and portable—which makes them easier to share, post, text, and embroider on a pillow.

Focus on an emotionally powerful endpoint and imagine it often. That emotional energy will make the reframe burrow into your mind faster.

What does it take to rewire a brain? Not much. You only need three things, and one of them is optional: Focus Repetition Emotion (fear, happiness, hate, love, passion, etc.)

I use this reframe a lot. The first thing I think when something falls apart is that an opportunity has been created. I still deal with the problem, of course, but I’ve rewired my brain via repetition to automatically look for the upside. I always find it.

If you learn to look for opportunities in chaos or in bad news of any kind, I guarantee you will spot some sooner or later. All you need to do is train yourself to look for the opportunities.

As I will often mention—no matter how much you hate it!—good reframes often have the weird quality of being untrue literally while still being useful.
