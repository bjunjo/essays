Democracy in America

by Alexis de Tocqueville, Harvey C. Mansfield, and Delba Winthrop

My note

Seeing democracy through Tocqueville’s eyes reminds me how freedom isn’t just about power, but about preserving individual independence within a complex society—something I think about often when balancing capital and sovereignty. His warning against centralized control and the loss of shared humanity resonates deeply, especially as I consider how Bitcoin challenges old hierarchies while fostering long-term freedom. This feels like a thoughtful reminder that greatness comes not from domination, but from thoughtful engagement with the systems we live in.
