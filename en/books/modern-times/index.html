My note

Reading *Modern Times* felt like tracing the anatomy of power itself—how absolute control twists ideals into tools of empire. It’s a sharp reminder that true sovereignty isn’t granted by any state or party, but earned through vigilance and long-term stewardship—whether of capital, ideas, or freedom. Thought you’d appreciate how it echoes the urgency behind thinking ₿ig while acting $mall in a world that so often centralizes power.
