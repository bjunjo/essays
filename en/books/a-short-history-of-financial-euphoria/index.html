A Short History of Financial Euphoria

by John Kenneth Galbraith

My note

There’s a strange comfort in seeing how history’s cycles of euphoria and crash replay, reminding me why patience and skepticism are essential in capital allocation. It’s a sharp lens on the human side of markets—how collective belief and denial fuel bubbles—something Bitcoin’s design tries to sidestep by cutting out the noise and focusing on long-term financial sovereignty. Thinking about this with you makes me appreciate the quiet discipline behind “acting small” even when the crowd goes wild.
