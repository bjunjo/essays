The SaaS Sales Method for Sales Development Representatives

by Jacco van der Kooij, Dan Smith, Winning by Design

My note

Understanding the subtle signals a customer sends before they even realize they need a solution feels a lot like spotting early signs of a paradigm shift — it’s all about patience and timing. This book’s approach to nurturing leads reminds me that real capital allocation isn’t just about choosing where to put money, but also when and how to engage thoughtfully over the long run. Hope you find its mindset as refreshing as I did.

When nurturing a customer, make sure your communication always provides something of value. Don’t “Check in” or “bubble up my last message” – if you’re only asking for a meeting, you are not giving them anything in exchange.

Sometimes a single action is not enough to turn a suspect into a prospect.

An alerted inbound lead is also referred to as an MQL where the SDR will interact with the prospect to try and book a meeting.

A new customer hears about you via word-of-mouth. These are the hottest leads! And they need to be properly managed, which means sometimes they should go straight to the Account Executive (AE) for selling

With this type of lead, all the development rep has to say is “How may I help you?”

The development rep needs to follow up promptly and schedule a discovery call.

We recommend that a nurtured lead should also be fed into the outbound process.

If you can have a big impact, priority and budget will follow. But your solution is not for everyone.

Don’t qualify your customer, but see if they are a good fit. Looking for fit is like asking if they have the right profile for you to impact (improve) their business.

SAL: Sales Accepted Lead, is a Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL) that has been reviewed and passed to the sales team for approval.

SQL: Sales Qualified Lead, a person who expresses interest during the sales process.

MQL: Marketing Qualified Lead, a person who expresses interest and fits the profile.

Prospect: A person who expresses interest online.

These qualification methods do not apply in a customer-centric approach because the customer gains no value during the qualification process, often leading to the customer being standoffish.
