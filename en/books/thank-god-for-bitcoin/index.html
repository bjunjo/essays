Thank God for Bitcoin

by Bitcoin and Bible Group, Jimmy Song, Gabe Higgins, Derek Waltchack, Robert Breedlove, J.M. Bush, Jul...

My note

Building a foundation that lasts feels more urgent than ever, and this book captures why Bitcoin isn’t just money—it’s a philosophy of value, sovereignty, and resilience. It reminds me that real wealth isn’t about flashy assets but about aligning capital with long-term purpose, something I hope we keep at the center of everything we build together.

My highlights

11 highlights from Kindle. These are the lines I stopped at.

Economists would say that stealing is zero-sum, that is, there is no net benefit to the collective. The thief wins only at the expense of others. In other words, work is creative while theft is destructive.

Work is what we do in order to create something of value for ourselves and others. Theft is the opposite; it is taking something of value from others.

All money is not created equal. Money is a tool, and tools are designed and made only after a need or objective has already been identified.

Money can buy houses, private jets, and even sports teams, but it can’t buy joy.

Many wealthy people get sick, are killed, or tragically take their own lives.

Money can’t guarantee safety, longevity, or happiness.

The way we spend money reveals what we love and what we fear. Money, in other words, expresses the values of the spender.

Charles Munger, Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway, once said, “Show me the incentive and I’ll show you the outcome.” The reason for the lack of basic understanding of the nature of money is simple: there are massive incentives in keeping money obscure, complex, and seemingly impossible to understand.

Embracing Bitcoin is like building an economy on a solid structure. It is better money with a limitless range of utility.

True excellence is only accomplished through the intent of being built to last.

Neither the luxurious façade nor the quality of material used to build the houses mattered. The difference only grew apparent when the storms came; it was at that moment the foundation was tested.
