How Not to Be Wrong

by Jordan Ellenberg

My note

Numbers don’t just tell you what happened—they shape how you think about what’s possible, which is why Jordan Ellenberg’s take on math feels so relevant beyond the classroom. It’s a reminder that understanding the *why* behind calculations helps me stay grounded in long-term clarity, whether I’m sizing up Bitcoin’s potential or just trying not to fool myself with numbers. Thought you’d appreciate how it blends rigor with the kind of mindset that values thinking big but acting small.
