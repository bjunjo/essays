The Moral Sayings of Publius Syrus

by Publilius Syrus, Darius Lyman (Translator)

My note

There’s something timeless about the way these sayings cut through noise and ask us to trust our own judgment—something I think about often when navigating capital and life in uncertain times. It’s a reminder that true wealth isn’t just numbers, but the integrity and wisdom we build quietly over time. Reading this felt like a nudge to keep aligning actions with values, even when it’s easier to follow the crowd.
