Noise

by Daniel Kahneman, Olivier Sibony, and Cass R. Sunstein

My note

Noise reminded me how much the quality of my decisions depends not just on the outcome, but on the process behind them—something crucial when allocating capital in a world full of uncertainty. It’s a good anchor for thinking about judgment as a skill to refine over time, not just a moment to nail, which feels especially relevant when betting long-term on paradigm shifts like Bitcoin. Hope you find its lessons as grounding as I do when the noise gets loud.
