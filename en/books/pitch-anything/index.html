Pitch Anything

by Oren Klaff

My note

Understanding how our brains really work when we pitch ideas feels like decoding human nature itself—something that’s crucial when navigating capital allocation and long-term bets like Bitcoin. Owning the frame and speaking to the primal part of the brain reminds me that influence isn’t just logic; it’s about connection and timing, which is something I’m always trying to get better at. Thought you might appreciate this angle as much as I do.

My highlights

10 highlights from Kindle. These are the lines I stopped at.

We are hardwired to be bad at pitching. It is caused by the way our brains have evolved. The fact that you are pitching your idea from the neocortex but it is being received by the other person’s croc brain is a serious problem.

Messages that are composed and sent by your young neocortex are received and processed by the other person’s old crocodile brain.

I learned from molecular biologist Craig Smucker that when we pitch something—an idea, product, deal, or whatever—the highest level of our brain, the neocortex, is doing the work. It’s the neocortex that is forming ideas, putting them into language, and presenting them. This is fairly intuitive.

that means you have to own the room with frame control, drive emotions with intrigue pings, and get to a hookpoint fairly quickly. (Details on those last two in a second.)

That’s because a great pitch is not about procedure. It’s about getting and keeping attention. And

You can do all those things as well as they can be done—and still not be convincing.

Collectively, I call these the STRONG method (you will learn about these soon).

setting the frame, telling the story, revealing the intrigue, offering the prize, nailing the hookpoint, and getting the decision.

(frames create context and relevance; as we will see, the person who owns the frame owns the conversation).

As it turns out, pitching is one of those business skills that depends heavily on the method you use and not how hard you try.
