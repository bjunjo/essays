Rome Was Not Built in a Day - The Story of the Roman People Vol. I

by Nanami SHIONO, Ronald Dore, and Steven Wills

My note

What struck me most is how Rome’s leaders balanced power and community, embodying the people’s will without claiming divine right—a reminder that lasting systems rely on shared belief and thoughtful structure, not just authority. It’s a powerful lens for thinking about sovereignty and the long game, whether in capital or life. Hope it sparks some of the same reflections.
