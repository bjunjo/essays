How Innovation Works

by Matt Ridley

My note

Innovation rarely arrives fully formed—it’s more like a slow dance of trial, error, and unexpected luck, much like how Bitcoin’s value and meaning have evolved over time. Seeing how ordinary people and imperfect ideas drive progress reminds me that true change often comes from the edges, not the top, which feels deeply connected to why I believe in long-term patience and financial sovereignty.
