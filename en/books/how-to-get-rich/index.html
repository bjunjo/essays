How to Get Rich

by Felix Dennis

My Note

Felix Dennis was one of the wealthiest self-made men in Britain, and this book is the most honest thing I've read about what wealth actually costs. He doesn't romanticize it. He talks about the loneliness, the loss of time, the things you give up. What stuck with me is that I don't want excessive wealth — I want enough freedom to think clearly and not spend my days in a job I didn't choose. Dennis draws that line better than anyone. I wish more people read this before they start chasing.
