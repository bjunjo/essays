The Great Mental Models

by Shane Parrish, Rhiannon Beaubien

My note

Thinking in mental models feels like building a toolkit for navigating uncertainty—something I lean on when allocating capital or holding Bitcoin through paradigm shifts. Popper’s idea of falsifiability reminds me to stay humble and test my beliefs, especially when betting long term on things that challenge conventional wisdom. This book is a quiet nudge to keep sharpening the lens through which I see both markets and life.
