Sell the Meeting

by Scott Channell

My note

Clarity on where to focus energy is everything, especially when capital and time are the currency. This book’s insistence on mastering the fundamentals before chasing the next shiny tactic feels a lot like Bitcoin’s slow, steady revolution—building trust and value through discipline, not hype. It’s a solid reminder that meaningful connections, whether in investing or outreach, come from knowing who truly matters and respecting their time.

My highlights

25 highlights from Kindle. These are the lines I stopped at.

To set appointments and discovery calls effectively you need a crystal clear vision of your top value highest probability targets.

Better closing ratios are determined, in part, by the quality of the prospects, the quality of the sales process, and the quality of the messaging.

You are making the calls anyway. You must have a strategy and make an effort to determine the potential worth of those you call, even when you do not speak to your target.

A very big part of appointment setting success is determining which targets are qualified or not — determining if they are of “A” value (your highest potential value range) or lie on the opposite side of the spectrum. You want to find out if they are “E” value, likely below an acceptable value range so that you waste no further resources on them. And of course, you have “B” (above average,) “C” (average) and “D” (below average, but acceptable) values in between.

Outreach to set appointments and discovery calls is not for everyone. It’s not for every business. It makes sense in some places and is foolish in others.

A sad reality is that there are a lot of really great accounts out there, buying from competitors unaware that you exist. They don’t know you.

Good clients are just waiting for your call. Great clients are searching their emails for messages from you. But you are nowhere to be found.

If you skip the basics, you are building on sand. If you follow the herd, you will get mediocrity at best; you will be doomed to again and again trying the latest “new” thing that changes it all or the “one thing” that will make all the difference. Your personal results needle won’t move. You will still be in the middle or near the bottom of the pack.

Build Upon The Basics And What Used To Work. Do It Better To Thrive In New Realities.

Why should they take the risk, again, that some unknown entity will be worth their time when fairly quickly they can let their fingers do the walking over their keyboard and find top-notch credible vendor options worth a meeting?

Why Should They Spend Time With You - A Person Or Company They Don’t Know?

Second, your prospects don’t trust you. They have had too much time wasted and been snookered too often with fake sincerity, overstated claims, and half-truths.

First, your best prospects are better informed. They are just a few keystrokes away from the information they need to solve their problems.

Doing The Basics Right Provides More Quantum Leaps Than “New” Strategies Or Technologies

The root cause of poor performance is often lack of mastery of the basics.

A “cold call” is part of the appointment setting process. It is not THE process. Using this resource, when you reach out you will be doing it right.

There are nuances to every project, but what drives success are the core strategies and behaviors acted upon consistently. Get your core engine up and running powerfully first.

Assuming that you have a quality offering, you are doing them a favor by doing so. Many of these buyers will make the wrong decision.

If You Have Other Marketing Tools, Use Them. If you have other options that will deliver the meetings you need, I recommend you put them in play. If you don’t have options, or if your options won’t deliver what you need then you will have to make calls.

Without a system to get shoulder to shoulder with top dogs within “A” type prospect opportunities, you spend a lot of time with mid-level “Seymours.” Managers who don’t drive decision-making and can’t cut checks.

No first meeting? No first sales conversation? No close.

Why a System for First Sales Conversations? Because when you can get a substantive first interaction with decision-makers, you can close accounts.
