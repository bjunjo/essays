The Autobiography of Andrew Carnegie and the Gospel of Wealth

by Andrew Carnegie and Gordon Hutner

My note

Carnegie’s insistence on using wealth as a tool for lasting impact rather than accumulation alone really resonates with how I think about capital — it’s not just about ownership, but responsibility and vision. His focus on self-reliance and single-minded dedication feels like a precursor to the kind of long-term, sovereignty-driven mindset that Bitcoin inspires. It’s a reminder that real wealth is about empowering others to find their own path, not just stacking dollars.
