The Family Office

by William I. Woodson and Edward V. Marshall

My note

Family offices feel like the ultimate expression of long-term thinking and intentional capital stewardship, balancing complexity with the need for simplicity and purpose. This book helped me see how tailoring that structure to fit one’s unique aspirations—not just wealth—makes all the difference. It’s a reminder that real sovereignty includes designing the right support system, not just amassing assets.
