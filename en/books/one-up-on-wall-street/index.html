One Up on Wall Street

by Peter Lynch

My note

There’s something quietly powerful about trusting insiders who put their own skin in the game—it’s a reminder that real conviction often looks like patience and simplicity, not hype. This book made me think about how capital allocation isn’t just numbers, but a long game of understanding people, businesses, and the subtle signals that hint at future shifts. It’s a perspective that feels surprisingly aligned with how I view Bitcoin and financial sovereignty: deep roots over flashy moves.
