Interviews With the Masters

by Robert Greene

My note

Changing course indirectly, like hacking your way through complexity, feels a lot like how I navigate capital allocation—spotting the “right kind of wrong” ideas and leaning in quietly but relentlessly. Greene’s take on focus and learning from mistakes reminds me that sovereignty isn’t about control but about mastering the art of subtle influence over time. Thought you’d appreciate how it blends strategy with that hacker mindset we both admire.
