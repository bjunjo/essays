Think on These Things

by Jiddu Krishnamurti

My note

True freedom starts with seeing yourself clearly, without the noise of wanting to be someone else—a lesson Krishnamurti captures so well here. It reminds me that long-term sovereignty, whether over capital or mind, depends less on chasing external markers and more on understanding what’s already inside. Thought you might appreciate this kind of quiet, radical honesty as much as I do.

The desire to change yourself breeds envy, jealousy; whereas, in the understanding of what you are, there is a transformation of what you are.

Grown-up people fight for exactly the same reason, only their toys are position, wealth and power.

The real function of education is not to turn you out to be a clerk, or a judge, or a prime minister, but to help you understand the whole structure of this rotten society and allow you to grow to freedom, so that you will break away and create a different society, a new world.

society is the relationship between you and me and others.

Without love there is no freedom; without love, freedom is merely an idea which has no value at all.

We must find out for ourselves what it means to love; because if we don’t love we can never be thoughtful, attentive; we can never be considerate.

To love is not to ask anything in return, not even to feel that you are giving something—and it is only such love that can know freedom.

So freedom and love go together. Love is not a reaction. If I love you because you love me, that is mere trade, a thing to be bought in the market; it is not love.

We don’t just love and leave it there, but we ask something in return; and in that very asking we become dependent.

freedom is not a reaction, and until we understand reaction and go beyond it, we are never free.

To be free we have to revolt against all inward dependence, and we cannot revolt if we don’t understand why we are dependent.

If, as you grow older, you depend emotionally on your parents, on your wife or husband, on a guru, or on some idea, there is already the beginning of bondage.

It is not the mere physical dependence on another which is so binding, but the inward, psychological dependence from which you derive so-called happiness; for when you depend on somebody in that way, you become a slave.

the dependence on another for one’s happiness.

To be free is not merely to do what you like, or to break away from outward circumstances which bind you, but to understand the whole problem of dependence.

We are nobodies, yet we also live on this earth, and we all have to live together.

the older people themselves often know nothing about all this. They have merely read about it in some book, and think they have understood it.

The child will find out about himself if the environment in which he lives helps him to do so.

However much I may try to become intelligent, my stupidity will remain.

what is important is to understand stupidity.

It comes into being only when you understand the total process of the mind—not the mind according to some philosopher or teacher, but your own mind. Your mind is the result of all humanity, and when you understand it you don’t have to study a single book, because the mind contains the whole knowledge of the past.

Intelligence is not knowledge. If you could read all the books in the world it would not give you intelligence. Intelligence is something very subtle; it has no anchorage.

an intelligent mind is one which is constantly learning, never concluding.

So freedom lies, not in trying to become something different, nor in doing whatever you happen to feel like doing, nor in following the authority of tradition, of your parents, of your guru, but in understanding what you are from moment to moment.

The function of education, then, is to help you from childhood not to imitate anybody, but to be yourself all the time. And this is a most difficult thing to do:

All that kind of thing gives us self-assurance, a sense of importance. We all want to be famous people—and the moment we want to be something, we are no longer free.

Don’t most of us want to be safe? Don’t we want to be told what marvellous people we are, how lovely we look, or what extraordinary intelligence we have?

freedom is really a state of mind in which there is no fear or compulsion, no urge to be secure.

to discover what is true demands freedom from tradition, which means freedom from all fears.

But most of us do not give our hearts to anything, and that is why we put this sort of question.

if you think something is important, really worth while, you give your heart to it irrespective of governments and the edicts of society—and then it will succeed.

it is very important to find out while you are young what it is you really love to do; and this is the only way to create a new society.

Attention comes when you are deeply interested in something, for then you love to find out all about it; then your whole mind, your whole being is there.

It is only when you are constantly inquiring, constantly observing, constantly learning, that you find truth, God, or love; and you cannot inquire, observe, learn, you cannot be deeply aware, if you are afraid. So the function of education, surely, is to eradicate, inwardly as well as outwardly, this fear that destroys human thought, human relationship and love.

Living safely generally means living in imitation and therefore in fear.

But you are not encouraged to do this; no one tells you to question, to find out for yourself what God is, because if you were to rebel you would become a danger to all that is false.

To live is to find out for yourself what is true, and you can do this only when there is freedom, when there is continuous revolution inwardly, within yourself.

It is very easy to conform to what your society or your parents and teachers tell you. That is a safe and easy way of existing; but that is not living, because in it there is fear, decay, death.

freedom, not just to do what we like, but to understand the whole process of living?

Most of us have fear in one form or another; and where there is fear there is no intelligence.

Most of us, as we grow older, become frightened; we are afraid of living, afraid of losing a job, afraid of tradition, afraid of what the neighbours, or what the wife or husband would say, afraid of death.

You know, it is really very important while you are young to live in an environment in which there is no fear.

Any form of ambition, spiritual or mundane, breeds anxiety, fear; therefore ambition does not help to bring about a mind that is clear, simple, direct, and hence intelligent.

Do you know what intelligence is? It is the capacity, surely, to think freely, without fear, without a formula, so that you begin to discover for yourself what is real, what is true; but if you are frightened you will never be intelligent.

What is the point of it all if in the process your mind becomes dull, weary, stupid? So, while you are young, must you not seek to find out what life is all about?
