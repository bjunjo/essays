World Order

by Henry Kissinger

My note

Understanding that no single power can shape the world alone really resonated with me—it’s a reminder that true sovereignty isn’t isolation, but navigating complex interdependence. This book made me think about how lasting order, much like sound capital allocation, requires patience, balance, and a shared commitment to limits that protect long-term stability. It’s a tough dance between cooperation and self-interest, something I’m always wrestling with in both markets and life.
