The Great Degeneration

by Niall Ferguson

My note

Institutions shape the game, and without strong rules, even the best ideas can falter—a reality that echoes deeply when I think about financial sovereignty and Bitcoin’s promise. Ferguson’s take on how political structures can stall growth reminds me why long-term capital allocation means betting not just on markets, but on the durability of the systems behind them. It’s a sharp reminder that real freedom comes from building and protecting frameworks, not just chasing returns.
