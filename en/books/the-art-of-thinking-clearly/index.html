The Art of Thinking Clearly

by Rolf Dobelli

My note

Clear thinking isn’t just a habit; it’s a muscle we have to train, especially when the noise around us pulls so hard at our attention and judgment. This book’s reminders about willpower limits and the traps of liking bias feel like a practical guide to keeping my capital—and my mind—aligned for the long game. It’s a quiet nudge to embrace “good enough” decisions with confidence, something I’m learning applies just as much to life as to allocating capital.
