Your Money or Your Life

by Vicki Robin, Joe Dominguez, Monique Tilford

My note

Understanding money as life energy reframes everything — it’s not just about accumulating assets, but about consciously choosing how much of my time I’m willing to trade. This book’s approach to “enough” resonates deeply with how I think about capital allocation and sovereignty: true wealth is freedom to focus long-term, not the endless chase for more. it’s a reminder that the most valuable portfolio we manage is our own life.

There is really only one reason to read any book about money: to give yourself the gift of a better life. Money is not really the thing you’re after—after all, would you lock yourself in a dark, silent box forever in exchange for becoming a billionaire?
