The Founder's Dilemmas

by Noam Wasserman

My note

The way Wasserman breaks down the invisible costs and “handcuffs” of staying an employee really hits home—reminds me why financial sovereignty isn’t just about money but about breaking those ties to build something on your own terms. It’s a sharp reminder that long-term thinking includes knowing when to step out and trust your network and skills to create new opportunities. Thought you’d appreciate how it connects to the mindset behind taking calculated leaps, whether in startups or capital allocation.
