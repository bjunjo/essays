Reinvent Yourself

by James Altucher

My note

Reinvention isn’t just a buzzword; it’s a necessity when you’re building your own path without the safety net of a traditional job. James Altucher’s take on sampling life like a buffet reminds me that financial sovereignty and long-term thinking require constant learning and adapting—not just sticking to one fixed role or mindset. Elsa, this book feels like a nudge to keep evolving quietly, over-delivering in small ways while holding on to the bigger vision.

My highlights

22 highlights from Kindle. These are the lines I stopped at.

Your legacy is not what you do. It’s what the people who you teach do.

This is your only chance. And your mentor has plenty of people around him. Over-deliver on everything. Look for every opportunity to over-deliver.

Some people are very smart and you can get a lot of value from them but they are very draining when they are in person.

Believe it or not, sometimes it’s just as good (often better) to read all of their materials rather than be directly mentored.

Also, quantity is important because there is never just one thing you have been placed on this Earth to do. Life is a buffet and not a fixed-price meal. Speaking for myself, I like to sample.

said, I have a technique here, which is to provide updates every three to four months. This has worked for me in about two or three cases where they have eventually gotten back in touch.

Every day I seek out mentors: people who have great experience to help me, particularly in areas I am new to, excited about, and know nothing about.

Learning never stops. Many people die at 25 but are not put in the coffin until 75. The learning stopped for them early.

Habits. It’s the 5x5 rule. You are not just the average of the five people around you. You’re the average of the five habits you do, the things you eat, the ideas you have, the content you consume, etc.

Improving relationships. Plus, minus, equal: Finding mentors to teach you. Finding the next generation to teach. Finding friends who build you up and challenge you.

Defining freedom in different ways (reducing expectations, increasing sources of income so no one source controls you).

Five years and you need to start learning new skills, practicing new efforts, trying on new careers for size.

When you have one source of income—for instance, a job—you are falling into the trap.

But you also have to look at an important data point: the IRS says the average multi-millionaire has seven different sources of income.

I always say it first has to come from within: physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual health. I.e., write down 10 ideas a day. Be around good people. Be grateful, etc.

YOU CAN’T RELY ON GOVERNMENT OR EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS. They are primarily designed to make the rich richer and the poor poorer.

It’s not Democrat versus Republican anymore. Trump is not even a Republican. And Sanders wasn’t a Democrat. It’s the small rich at the top of the pyramid and the huge masses of poor at the bottom, and they will eventually win.

You’re going to make mistakes, but 80% is always good enough. Keep learning the next thing.

Every day, be creative. Creativity is a muscle.

Health is the most important thing, else your body today won’t let you enjoy tomorrow.

Life is a billion times smaller than the point of a needle. Don’t waste it doing things you were told to do. Do the things you love to do.

Only be around people you love and who inspire you.
