Confessions of a Wall Street Analyst

by Daniel Reingold and Jennifer Reingold

My note

Wall Street’s tangled dance between integrity and influence feels eerily familiar when you think about true financial sovereignty—how much of what we rely on is shaped by incentives, not truth. This book reminded me that long-term capital allocation demands relentless vigilance and a willingness to question the system, even when it’s dressed up as expertise. It’s a sharp nudge to keep thinking ₿ig but always acting with quiet discipline.
