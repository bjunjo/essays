Market Wizards, Updated

by Jack D. Schwager

My note

Risk and patience are the real currencies here—reminds me how capital allocation isn’t about chasing every signal but waiting for the right moment with conviction. This book’s emphasis on discipline and understanding leverage feels close to how I think about Bitcoin and financial sovereignty: long-term moves, thoughtful risks, and staying clear of noise.

Guaranteed by exchange—The futures trader does not have to be concerned about the financial stability of the person on the other side of the trade.

Ease of offset—A futures position can be offset at any time during market hours, providing prices are not locked at limit-up or limit-down.

Low transaction costs—Futures markets provide very low transaction costs.

The high-risk reputation of futures is largely a consequence of the leverage factor.

general, futures prices are no more volatile than the underlying cash prices or, for that matter, many stocks.

The undisciplined use of leverage is the single most important reason why most traders lose money in the futures markets.

leverage is a two-edged sword.

(The use of the term margin in the futures market is unfortunate because it leads to tremendous confusion with the concept of margins in stocks. In the futures markets, margins do not imply partial payments, since no actual physical transaction occurs until the expiration date; rather, margins are basically good-faith deposits.)

Leverage—The futures markets offer tremendous leverage. Roughly speaking, initial margin requirements are usually equal to 5 to 10 percent of the contract value.

Ease of going short—The futures markets allow equal ease of going short as well as long.

Liquidity—All of the major futures markets provide excellent liquidity.

thus, the trader does not have to find a specific buyer or seller in order to initiate or liquidate a position.
