Fail Fast, Fail Often

by Ryan Babineaux and John Krumboltz

My note

Fail Fast, Fail Often reminds me that real growth comes from small, imperfect moves rather than waiting for the “perfect” plan—something I try to apply when balancing long-term capital allocation with staying nimble. It’s a good nudge to keep experimenting and adapting, whether in ideas or life, without overcommitting before learning what really works. Thought you’d appreciate how it ties into the mindset of thinking big but acting small.
