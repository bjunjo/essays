Why I'm Betting on Nakamoto (NAKA) at $0.215

A personal investment thesis on the most asymmetric bet in Bitcoin treasury stocks.

I've spent the last year tracking every Bitcoin treasury company on the planet. Strategy, Metaplanet, Smarter Web Company, H100 Group — I watch their filings, their mNAV ratios, their accumulation patterns. I built a dashboard for it. I get alerts on their disclosures every morning.

So when I tell you NAKA at $0.215 is the most interesting risk/reward I've seen in this space, I'm not guessing. I'm comparing.

The Setup

Nakamoto Inc. (NAKA) is a Bitcoin treasury company listed on NASDAQ. They hold 5,058 BTC. Their stock is down 99% from its all-time high. The market is pricing them at 0.96x mNAV — meaning the market thinks their operating businesses (Bitcoin Magazine, Bitcoin Conference, UTXO Management) are worth exactly zero.

Zero. For the company that runs the biggest Bitcoin media platform in the world.

Why It's Cheap

NAKA is going through a hard time. They sold 284 BTC at a 40% loss to fund operations. They have a $210M loan from Kraken at 8% interest. They're burning cash. The stock is a penny stock.

But here's what the market is missing: the operating businesses are real. Bitcoin Magazine isn't a shell company. Bitcoin Conference isn't a SPAC. These are revenue-generating businesses with defensible moats in the Bitcoin ecosystem. The market is pricing them at zero because NAKA is struggling right now. That's the opportunity.

Why mNAV Converges to 1x

Before I get into the math, I need to explain something about mNAV that most people misunderstand.

There's a borrowed idea from James Check at Checkonchain that I think is critical: all Bitcoin treasury company mNAVs converge towards 1x over time.

Here's why. A Bitcoin treasury company's value is backed by a finite asset — Bitcoin. There are only 21 million coins that will ever exist. As a treasury company accumulates more BTC, the market starts asking a simple question: "Why am I paying a premium for this company when I could just buy BTC directly?"

The premium exists for two reasons: (1) the operating businesses generate revenue that compounds independently of BTC, and (2) the company can raise capital to buy more BTC at scale. But as more treasury companies emerge and competition increases, the premium compresses. The market gets more efficient at pricing BTC exposure.

Strategy (MSTR) traded at 3x mNAV during the 2024 euphoria. Today it's around 2x. Metaplanet was at 4x, now closer to 1.5x. The direction is clear: mNAV drifts towards 1x.

This is why I use 1x as my base case, not 2x or 3x. The premium is temporary. The discount is temporary. Over a full cycle, the market settles at 1x — where treasury value equals equity value. If you get a premium, great. But don't count on it.

At 0.96x, NAKA is already trading below the efficient market baseline. That means the market is giving you BTC at a 4% discount. Even if mNAV never goes above 1x, you're still getting a better deal than buying BTC directly.

Why I Believe They Won't Stop at 5K BTC

The obvious objection is: "NAKA can't raise $10-12B to accumulate 25K BTC. They can barely keep the lights on."

Here's why I disagree:

1. The ATM infrastructure is real. NAKA has a $5B ATM program filed with 10 banks. They've only used $6.4M so far. The reason? The stock price is $0.215. ATM programs don't work at penny stock prices — they work when the stock is $5, $10, $20. The infrastructure is in place. It just needs a higher price to activate.

2. The flywheel is designed to compound. Bitcoin Magazine generates revenue. Bitcoin Conference generates revenue. UTXO Management generates fees. These aren't vanity projects — they're cash-flowing businesses that fund BTC acquisition. As they integrate and optimize, the cash flow grows. More cash flow → more BTC purchases → higher stock price → more ATM capacity → more BTC purchases. That's the flywheel.

3. David Bailey's network is the moat. This is the guy who built Bitcoin for Corporations. He has relationships with every major Bitcoin institutional player. When the ATM program needs to activate, he has the network to make it happen. You don't build a $5B ATM with 10 banks by accident.

4. The math works at any scale. Even if NAKA only gets to 10K BTC (not 25K), the return is 30-60x at 1-2x mNAV. Even if they only get to 8K BTC, it's 25-50x. The accumulation doesn't need to be perfect. It just needs to be directionally correct.

5. The market will reprice before the accumulation is done. If NAKA shows 8K BTC by 2028 and the operating businesses are profitable, the stock will be $5-10, not $0.215. At $5-10, the ATM program works beautifully. They can raise $500M per quarter with minimal dilution. The early accumulation is the hardest part. After that, the flywheel takes over.

The Math That Matters

Let's say you put $10,000 in.

  • Worst case: You lose $10,000. Painful, but survivable.
  • Base case (BTC $1M, no accumulation): $254,000 (25x)
  • Bull case (BTC $1M, 25K BTC, 2x mNAV): $1,550,000 (155x)

That's a 50-150:1 payoff ratio. Venture capitalists would kill for those odds.

Why NAKA and Not Just Bitcoin?

If you just hold Bitcoin and it goes to $1M, you get 13x from here. That's great. But NAKA gives you 2-12x more leverage through two mechanisms:

  1. mNAV discount. NAKA trades at 0.96x. You're buying BTC at a 4% discount. As the market re-rates to 1-2x, you get extra return for free.
  2. Accumulation. If NAKA successfully raises capital and buys more BTC, every coin they buy is worth $1M at your target. Going from 5K to 25K BTC is a 5x multiplier on top of the BTC price appreciation.

The Risks (I'm Not Going to Pretend They Don't Exist)

Kraken loan. They have $210M in debt at 8% interest. If BTC drops significantly, they could face margin pressure. This is the biggest risk.

Dilution. They need to raise $10-12B over 6 years to accumulate 25K BTC. At current prices, that's extremely dilutive. The stock price needs to rise for dilution to be manageable.

Execution. David Bailey has to actually build a profitable Bitcoin conglomerate. The operating businesses need to generate enough cash to reduce reliance on BTC sales. So far, they've sold BTC at a loss, not accumulated.

Survival. They have 6 months of cash runway. They need to raise capital or generate revenue soon. If they go bankrupt before 2032, the thesis is dead.

Why I'm Betting Anyway

I've tracked these companies for a year. I understand the mNAV framework, the dilution mechanics, the Kraken loan structure. I know what I'm buying.

The reason I'm comfortable sizing this at $10K-$15K is because the math works even if NAKA executes poorly. Even if they can't raise capital, even if they sell some BTC to survive, even if the operating businesses barely break even — if Bitcoin goes to $1M, I get 25x.

The 78-155x upside is gravy. The base case is 25x. And the only way I lose everything is if NAKA goes bankrupt or Bitcoin doesn't reach $1M.

I believe Bitcoin reaches $1M by 2032. That's my core conviction. NAKA is just the most leveraged way to express it.

The Bottom Line

NAKA at $0.215 is a lottery ticket with real odds. The downside is $10K. The upside is $780K-$1.55M. The math works even in the worst case.

I'm not betting on David Bailey executing perfectly. I'm betting on Bitcoin going to $1M. NAKA is just the vehicle.

If you agree that Bitcoin goes to $1M by 2032, ask yourself: what's the most leveraged way to express that bet? At 0.96x mNAV, with 5,058 BTC, down 99% from highs — NAKA is the answer.
