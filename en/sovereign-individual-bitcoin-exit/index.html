The Sovereign Individual thesis — Bitcoin is the exit from the nation-state

I first read The Sovereign Individual in 2021.

I first read The Sovereign Individual in 2021. Written in 1997, it nailed something most missed: the internet would erode the nation-state’s grip on power and money. When the cost of protecting an institution outweighs its value, that institution collapses. The Catholic Church lost its grip in the Middle Ages. Today, governments face the same problem.

Bitcoin is the financial tool that makes this theory real. Unlike anything before, it can’t be seized, inflated, or taxed without your say-so. It’s the exit hatch from the traditional nation-state financial system.

But Bitcoin alone isn’t enough. The real escape kit is Bitcoin plus Starlink — money plus connectivity. Starlink keeps the internet alive even when traditional infrastructure is destroyed. The wars in Ukraine and the Iran-Israel conflict proved this. Civilians used Bitcoin to move money when banks froze, and Starlink kept them connected. This isn’t theory anymore; it’s reality.

"Anyone with a portable computer and a satellite link will be able to conduct almost any information business anywhere, and that includes almost the whole of the world's multitrillion-dollar financial transactions." — James Dale Davidson & William Rees-Mogg, 1997

Most people still treat Bitcoin as a speculative gamble. It isn’t. It’s political technology — insurance, not rebellion. Governments do vital work: defense, police, infrastructure. I served in the Korean military. I respect that. Bitcoin is a backup plan for when the system strains or breaks, not a call to burn it down.

People worry governments will ban Bitcoin. History says otherwise. When technology threatens institutional revenue, institutions fight — and lose. Just look at the Soviet Union trying to block PCs and Xerox machines. It failed.

"Power… has always sought the readiest road to wealth, by attacking those who were in possession of it." — Davidson & Rees-Mogg

The nation-state is in its twilight. Debt piles up, currencies weaken, and capital flees. Bitcoin is the exit ramp. Pair it with Starlink, a second passport, and flexible residence, and you become a Sovereign Individual. Bitcoin is the foundation — without portable wealth, the rest are just ideas.

What might go wrong? Maybe governments adapt and co-opt Bitcoin, or the transition gets violent. But recent conflicts show the opposite: war speeds Bitcoin adoption. When traditional channels freeze, Bitcoin becomes necessity, not luxury.

In ten years, this thesis will be mainstream. Today’s Bitcoin holders are the pioneers of financial freedom, like those moving capital offshore before the last century’s great confiscations. It’s not about getting rich. It’s about staying free.

Davidson and Rees-Mogg predicted in 1997 the tools of sovereignty would be a portable computer and satellite link. Today it’s your smartphone and Starlink. Bitcoin runs the financial transactions. They weren’t early. They were exactly on time.

Bitcoin isn’t a gamble. It’s a lifeboat.
